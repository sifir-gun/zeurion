# Sponsor Prospecting Report : Detection Engineering Weekly

Prepared: July 21, 2026. Research window: April-July 2026, core coverage June 20 - July 20, 2026. All claims in this report link to a public, dated source. No contact has been made with any brand on your behalf.

---

## Executive summary

Your newsletter reaches roughly 15,000 detection engineers, SOC analysts, and threat intel practitioners every week, across 163 issues, and currently carries zero sponsorships. Meanwhile, the newsletters and podcasts your audience also reads sold out their sponsor slots: CloudSecList (12k subscribers) charges $1,950 per placement and reports 2025 fully booked, with reservations already extending into late 2026 (https://cloudseclist.com/sponsor).

This report identifies **30 brands currently paying for sponsorships in front of audiences comparable to yours**, each with dated, linked evidence of their spend, the person most likely to own that budget, and a ready-to-send pitch. Ten of them (tier 1) bought multiple placements across several security newsletters in the last six weeks alone.

Method credibility check: two of the newsletters we scanned publish their past sponsor lists. Cross-referencing our extraction against Vulnerable U's declared sponsors, we independently re-found 11 of 12 as currently active buyers elsewhere (92%). The method finds live budgets, not stale logos.

The math: at the recommended $2,000-2,500 primary placement rate, **one closed sponsor from this list covers the cost of this report several times over**. Four booked slots a month at the low end represent roughly $8,000/month of currently unmonetized inventory.

Recommended first three calls: **Tines** (7+ placements across 4 newsletters in 5 weeks, including your closest peers), **Sublime Security** (bought a full week of Risky Business inventory in early July), **Nebulock** (paid for a wholly sponsored Risky Business Soap Box episode about threat hunting, the exact center of your audience).

## How this report was built

- We scanned the public archives of 14+ security newsletters and podcasts your audience overlaps with: tl;dr sec, Vulnerable U, Resilient Cyber, Return on Security, Risky Business (Bulletin, Srsly, Soap Box), Unsupervised Learning, CloudSecList, Venture in Security, CISO Series (Defense in Depth, Cyber Security Headlines), Darknet Diaries, Security Weekly, and others.
- 103 raw sponsorship observations were recorded, each with the brand, the issue or episode, the date, the format (primary / secondary / dedicated / mention), and the URL.
- Brands were ranked by editorial alignment with a detection engineering audience, recency and recurrence of spend (recurrence = active budget), and format purchased.
- Contacts were identified from public sources only: official team pages, press releases, conference bios, public LinkedIn pages. Nothing was scraped from behind a login, and **no email address in this report was guessed**: published addresses are marked as such; everything else is marked "verify before sending" and should be run through an email verification tool.

## Your pricing: what the market will bear

No comparable newsletter publishes a full rate card except CloudSecList, which is why it anchors this recommendation:

| Benchmark | Audience | Price | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| CloudSecList | 12,000+ cloud security subscribers | $1,950 per placement, 2 slots/issue, volume discount from 4 slots, sold out 2025 | https://cloudseclist.com/sponsor |
| Vulnerable U | 32,000+ subscribers, ~44% open rate | Rates on request; two tiers (primary/secondary) plus social and video upsells | https://www.vulnu.com/c/sponsors |
| Resilient Cyber | 31,287 subscribers, 46% open rate | Rates on request; featured slot plus sponsored podcast interview | https://www.resilientcyber.io/sponsorships |
| Venture in Security | 20,000+ subscribers, 30-40% open rate | Rates on request; single text placement per article | https://ventureinsecurity.net/p/sponsor |

CloudSecList sells at roughly $0.16 per subscriber per placement to an audience very close to yours in specialization. Applied to your 15,000 highly targeted subscribers:

- **Primary placement (top of issue, 400-600 characters, one CTA link): $2,000-2,500.** Justified above the per-subscriber benchmark by the specialization of your audience and the fact that your inventory is untouched: no sponsor fatigue.
- **Secondary placement (lower in issue, shorter): $900-1,200.**
- **Classified / one-line mention: $350-450.**
- **Bundle: 4 primary slots for the price of 3.2** (20% volume discount), matching the segment norm of multi-issue purchases.

Standard segment packaging, observed identically across the four titles above: two slots per issue (primary + secondary), with upsells (social promotion, podcast interview, full-week takeover as sold by Risky Business). Start with primary + classified only; add tiers as demand proves out.

One data point to add yourself before pitching: your open rate. Your peers publish theirs (44% Vulnerable U, 46% Resilient Cyber, ~50% CloudSecList) and sponsors will ask. Substack shows it in your stats dashboard; if it is above 40%, put it in every pitch.

## Your mini media kit: the five numbers that sell

1. **15,000 subscribers**, and say it precisely: sponsors discount round numbers they cannot verify. If you can screenshot your Substack dashboard in the pitch thread, do it.
2. **Weekly since 2022, 163 consecutive issues**: reliability is what media buyers price.
3. **Who reads you**: detection engineers, SOC leads, threat intel analysts, the exact buyer persona for detection, SIEM, SOAR, and endpoint tooling. Name the job titles, not "security professionals".
4. **Zero sponsors to date**: virgin inventory, no banner blindness, first movers get category exclusivity if you choose to offer it.
5. **Your open rate and CTR** (from your dashboard): the segment norm among your peers is 40-50% open; if you beat it, lead with it.

Two follow-ups, then stop. The audience you are selling to (marketing leaders in security) is oversolicited; restraint is a signal.

Both remain worth a pitch, with expectations set accordingly.

- Observations cover a 6-week core window. A brand absent from this report is not proven inactive; a brand present is proven active.
